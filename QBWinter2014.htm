qb courses 2014

AMATH 900 Mathematical Biology (Spring 2014)

This course will introduce a range of quantitative research methods that are currently used in the biological sciences. Topics include differential equation modelling, agent-based modelling, bioinformatics tools, optimization, and stochastic processes. This is a team-taught course, with instructors from Applied Math, Biology, Computer Science, and Pharmacy. The course will be delivered in two-week modules. Class meetings, held Tues./Thurs. 2:00-3:30, will be a combination of lectures and journal-club style discussions.
Background: the course is designed for an interdisciplinary audience. Students will be expected to be comfortable with mathematics, programming, and biology.
Assessment: Students will deliver journal-club presentations and a term project.

BIOL 382/AMATH 382 Computational Modeling of Cellular Systems (Spring 2014)

An introduction to dynamic mathematical modeling of cellular processes. The emphasis is on using computational tools to investigate differential equation-based models. A variety of cellular phenomena are discussed, including ion pumps, membrane potentials, intercellular communication, genetic networks, regulation of metabolic pathways, and signal transduction.


BIOL 364 Mathematical Modelling in Biology (Fall 2014)

An introduction to the mathematical modelling of biological processes using a variety of techniques including linear difference models, ordinary differential equations, and Markov models. Modelling applications ranging from genetics to ecosystem biology will be addressed.
