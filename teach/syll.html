Course description

We will survey the field of ecology starting with background in individual, population, community, evolutionary and ecosystem ecology. We will simultaneously examine new developments in selected areas dependent on student interest.

Winter 2023: Topic of interest is population modelling with an emphasis on stage-structured models

Course learning objectives:

Content objectives

  1. List and explain major concepts in one area of ecology,

  2. Explain major new developments in one area of ecology

Skill objectives

  1. Develop writing skills

  2. Develop mathematical, statistical and computation skills necessary for population modelling

  3. Learn how to provide open science documentation for population modelling

Course structure

Level 1 Introduction to modelling and simple unstructured models, including the role of stochasticity

Level 2 Introduction and application of simple structured population models (matrix models), ), including stochasticity

Level 3 Integral projection models for structured population modelling, including stochasticity

Level 4 Transients and reactivity in structured population models

Description of Graded Assessments

OER presentation - lead: An oral presentation to the class providing an overview of the topic area including some materials that will be included in the OER project, identification of key papers, and selection of recent paper, overview of R resources for the topic, bibliography). Format is a 50 min lecture appropriate to a 4th year undergraduate or 1st graduate course, followed by a paper discussion, and ideas for the class contribution to the OER product

OER presentation - supporting: An oral presentation to the class providing an overview of the topic area including some materials that will be included in the OER project, identification of key papers, and selection of recent paper, overview of R resources for the topic, bibliography). Format is a 20 min lecture appropriate to a 4th year undergraduate or 1st graduate course, followed by a paper discussion, and ideas for the class contribution to the OER product

OER outline - lead: Each student will submit a written outline of the OER presentation that can be incorporated in the course OER materials (.md or .rmd format). This material will cover major concepts in the area (~2 pages), a classical reading (~1 page), and a new reading (~1page), R resources and a bibliography. References must be supplied as required, and the document must compile without errors. Maximum of 2 pages in .pdf format (500 words) due 17:00, 1 weeks after the initial presentation.

OER materials - lead: Each student will combine supporting and original materials in a teaching unit (.md or .rmd format). This material will cover major concepts in the area (~2 pages), a classical reading and a new reading, R resources and a bibliography. References must be supplied as required, and the document must compile without errors. Maximum of 5 pages in .pdf format (1500 words) due 17:00, 2 weeks after the initial presentation. Final grade for this section will be related to the quality of all submitted materials (including those created by the supporting team), but the lead will have the opportunity to both provide feedback to supporting members, and to revise the materials themselves in accordance with instructor feedback.

OER materials - supporting: Each student will submit written materials for each of the 3 remaining OER sections as requested by the project lead (.md or .rmd format). This material may cover major concepts in the area, readings, mathematics or programming, and R resources. References must be supplied as required, and document must compile without errors. Should be about 2 pages in .pdf format due 17:00, 2 weeks after the initial presentation. Supporting members will have the opportunity to revise the materials based on feedback from the lead and instructor.
